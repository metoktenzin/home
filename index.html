What is Chess

Chess is a popular board game that is played on a worldwide scale but is played the most in Russia. It originates from India, with its first traces dating back to up to 1500 years ago. Chess is a competitive game that is played between two players and requires both strategy and skill. Although this greatly varies, each game usually has an average length of 10-20 minutes. These games are played on a checkerboard and include 32 pieces that are evenly distributed amongst the players.

How the Pieces Work ♖ ♔ ♘

Although chess pieces may seem intimidating at first, they are quite simple to learn. Each player is dealt 16 pieces of 6 different types. These include: the king, queen, bishop, rook, knight, and pawn. Each of these 6 types of pieces move in their own unique way across the chessboard! First, is the king. Even though this is the most important piece in the game, it’s one of the weakest pieces, as its movement is very restricted. This proves true, as the king can only move within a one square radius in any direction.

Second, is the rook. The rook can move in straight lines in any direction, this can be done for any amount of squares. Another piece is the bishop. This piece moves diagonally in any direction and also doesn’t have a limit for the number of squares that can be moved.

The next piece is the pawn. The pawn can move up to 2 squares in its first move, but can only move 1 square for the remainder of the match. It eliminates other pieces that are diagonally one piece in front of them and can change into any other piece excluding itself and the king if it successfully moves to the end of the other side of the board. The Knight is a very unique piece because, unlike the other pieces, it can move over other pieces in an enclosed area. It moves in 3 spots straight and one spot to the left in any direction, forming an L shape. Last, but not least, is the queen, which is a very powerful piece on the board. It doesn’t have one set movement pattern but instead utilizes both the bishop’s and rook’s moving pattern.

How to Play

In Chess, the main objective is to checkmate your opponent’s king. Checkmating occurs when the king is in a position where in the next move the opposing team can eliminate this piece, and it has to try to get in a position where this cannot happen. When you checkmate them and they cannot escape an attack with any moves in their next move, then you win. The easiest way to make this happen is by eliminating as many other pieces as possible. By doing this, you leave a few pieces of your opponent that are able to defend with.

Standard Rules

Like all games, some rules that should be followed. Firstly, the player with white pieces must always make the first move. After they move, each player takes a turn to move one piece each time, and they must only move during their turn. When moving a piece, you must only move your pieces unless you are eliminating one of your opponent's pieces.

Types of Chess

Although we normally focus on the original version of chess, other versions are played significantly more depending on the location. For example, one that also originates in India is Shatranj. This is an old form of chess that isn’t as popular as it was in its time era, which was roughly in 600 AD. This form of chess is quite similar to the original version, as the only difference was that there were different pieces, but they had relatively similar functions to our now modern chess. Chess Players and Ratings

Throughout history, there have been a number of renowned chess players that have made an impact on chess forever. All of these players have different levels that distinguish them apart and show their standings.

Levels

In chess, there are different levels depending on your rating. Usually, beginners have lower ratings and more experienced players have higher ratings. These are used to determine who you match with in tournaments and other competitive matches.
