What is Chess

Chess is a popular board game that is played on a worldwide scale, but is played the most in Russia. It is a competitive game that is played between two players, and requires both strategy and skill. Although this greatly varies, each game usually has an average length of 10-20 minutes. These games are played on a checkerboard and include 32 pieces that are evenly distributed amongst the players.

How the Pieces Work ♖ ♔ ♘

Although chess pieces may seem intimidating at first, they are really quite simple to learn. Each player is dealt 16 pieces of 6 different types. These include: the king, queen, bishop, rook, knight, and pawn. Each of these 6 types of pieces move in their own unique way across the chess board! First, is the king. Even though this is the most important piece in the game, it's one of the weakest pieces, as it's movement is very restricted. This proves true, as the king can only move within a one square radius to any direction. The next piece, is the rook. The rook can move in straight lines in any direction, this can be done for any amount of squares. Another piece, is the bishop. This piece moves diagonally in any directions and also doesn’t have a limit for the amount of squares that can be moved.

How to Play

In Chess, the main objective is to checkmate the king. Checkmating occurs when the king is cannot escape an attack with any moves.

Standard Rules

Like all games, there are rules that should be followed. Firstly, the player with white pieces must always make the first move. After they move, each player takes a turn to move one piece each time, and they must only move during their turn. When moving a piece, you must only move your pieces unless you are eliminating one of your opponent's pieces.

Where to Start

After all this information, you are probably feeling quite overwhelmed and might not know where to start in the world of chess, but not to worry! Nowadays there are many new players discovering chess, so you aren’t alone. The easiest way to start, is practicing with people who are around your level. could be a friend, a sibling, a parent, basically anyone you know. You can also do this online, as there are many good chess websites that can be used. An example of one, is Chess Online.
